  • 01
    Impedance Matching
    ZnO and the graded dielectric constant of the RAM layer minimises surface reflection. The wave enters the coating with minimal bounce-back, allowing deep penetration for absorption.
  • 02
    Magnetic Loss (µ″)
    Carbonyl iron and barium ferrite exhibit high imaginary permeability, dissipating energy via magnetic domain resonance at GHz frequencies.
  • 03
    Dielectric Loss (ε″)
    Iron oxide contributes polarisation and conduction losses, converting residual EM energy into thermal energy within the matrix.
  • 04
    Thermal Signature Suppression
    IR-reflective pigments in the topcoat reduce emissivity, masking the thermal signature from IR-guided sensors and thermal cameras.

MATERIAL SCIENCE

Materials Used

CARBONYL IRON
Fe (Carbonyl)
Primary radar absorber. High saturation magnetisation and tunable permeability across 1–18 GHz. Spherical particles for isotropic absorption.

Effectiveness: 85%

BARIUM FERRITE
BaFe₁₂O₁₉
Hexagonal ferrite with hard magnetic properties. High natural resonance frequency (45–55 GHz) makes it ideal for mmWave and X-band absorption.

Effectiveness: 78%

IRON OXIDE
Fe₂O₃ / Fe₃O₄
Dielectric loss contribution. Acts as a secondary absorber and enhances matrix stability. Provides broadband coverage via interfacial polarisation.

Effectiveness: 65%

ZINC OXIDE
ZnO
Impedance matching agent. Tunes the complex permittivity of the composite to minimise surface reflection and maximise wave penetration depth.

Effectiveness: 72%

EPOXY RESIN
Epoxy (Primer)
Two-part thermoset binder. Provides exceptional adhesion to metallic substrates, corrosion resistance, and mechanical stability under vibration and thermal cycling.

Adhesion rating: 90%

POLYASPARTIC
Polyaspartic Resin
Aliphatic polyurea-based topcoat. UV-stable, fast-cure (2–4 hrs), extreme weather resistance (−40°C to +120°C), and excellent hardness.

Durability: 95%
